Case Study

Deep dive: Restaurant Data Analysis Dashboard.

Problem

Restaurant listings contain useful market signals, but the patterns are hard to see when pricing, cuisine, service options, and customer ratings are spread across raw rows. The goal was to build a structured view of what combinations were linked to stronger customer response across locations.

Cleaning

  • Standardized city, locality, and cuisine values so similar entries were grouped correctly in reporting.
  • Reviewed missing or weak values in ratings, votes, and service fields before creating KPIs.
  • Prepared pricing and delivery variables for cleaner segmentation across restaurant groups.
  • Used Power Query to shape the model for reliable filtering and comparison inside Power BI.

Analysis

  • Built a multi-page Power BI dashboard to compare cuisines, price bands, ratings, and service options.
  • Used DAX measures to define KPIs and make segment-level comparisons easier to interpret.
  • Analyzed how delivery availability, cuisine type, and pricing aligned with ratings and customer engagement.

Insights

  • Delivery-enabled restaurants showed stronger rating patterns than comparable non-delivery segments.
  • Mid-range pricing appeared more attractive than higher-cost tiers when customer preference was compared across groups.
  • Performance patterns varied by cuisine and geography, which means one market view would have been misleading.
  • Interactive filtering helped isolate where service model, pricing, and cuisine overlapped to produce better response.

Recommendations

  • Benchmark strong cuisine-price combinations before entering a new locality or adjusting positioning.
  • Treat delivery as a strategic lever, not just an operational feature, when evaluating customer demand.
  • Review pricing decisions at the city or locality level rather than assuming one preference pattern applies everywhere.
  • Use dashboard filters to support targeted comparisons before making service expansion decisions.

Business Value

  • The output turns a broad restaurant dataset into a decision tool for pricing, market comparison, and service strategy.
  • It demonstrates an analyst workflow from cleaning and modeling to insight generation and action-oriented recommendations.
  • It also shows the ability to move beyond descriptive charts into analysis tied to specific business questions.
