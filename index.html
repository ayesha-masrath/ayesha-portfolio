Case Study

Deep dive: Restaurant Data Analysis Dashboard.

Problem

Restaurant performance is shaped by several variables at once, including cuisine mix, pricing, ratings, and delivery preference. The goal was to convert a broad restaurant dataset into a dashboard that helps users compare performance drivers instead of scanning raw listings.

Data cleaning steps

  • Standardized city, locality, and cuisine fields to reduce inconsistent labels.
  • Reviewed missing or weak values in ratings, votes, and service availability columns.
  • Prepared pricing and service fields for segmentation analysis.
  • Used Power Query to shape the dataset for Power BI modeling and interactive filtering.

Analysis approach

  • Built an interactive Power BI dashboard to analyze cuisines, pricing, ratings, and delivery preferences.
  • Used DAX measures to define KPIs and comparison logic across restaurant segments.
  • Connected delivery, pricing, and customer ratings to understand what patterns were associated with stronger response.

Visualizations

  • KPI summary cards for restaurant performance at a glance.
  • City and locality comparisons to identify high-activity segments.
  • Cuisine and price-range views to compare customer preference patterns.
  • Interactive filters and comparison views for service-level analysis.

Insights

  • Ratings and engagement varied meaningfully across cuisines and city segments.
  • Pricing bands helped reveal where value perception and customer response aligned most strongly.
  • Delivery preferences created a useful lens for comparing convenience-driven demand.
  • Combined filters made it easier to compare restaurant performance across service model, cuisine, and geography.

Final recommendations

  • Benchmark top-performing cuisine and pricing combinations before launching new strategies.
  • Use delivery and rating trends together when assessing service expansion opportunities.
  • Compare city-level performance separately instead of assuming one customer pattern fits every market.
  • Use dashboard filters to move from broad market review into localized decision-making faster.
